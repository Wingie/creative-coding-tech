üìñ Lectio Divina: Sacred Reading

Praying Scripture with the Church Fathers

The Four Movements

  • Lectio (Read): Read slowly, attentively, expectantly
  • Meditatio (Meditate): Reflect, chew, digest the Word
  • Oratio (Pray): Respond to God from your heart
  • Contemplatio (Contemplate): Rest silently in God's presence

Historical Roots

From the Desert Fathers through St. Benedict's Rule, this 1,500-year-old practice transformed Scripture from information into formation.

üïäÔ∏è Centering Prayer: Resting in Presence

The Prayer of Consent

The Practice

  • Choose a sacred word (Jesus, Abba, Peace, Love)
  • Sit in silence, consenting to God's presence
  • When thoughts arise, gently return to your sacred word
  • Rest in God's loving presence for 20 minutes

Theological Foundation

"Be still and know that I am God" (Psalm 46:10). This contemplative practice recovers the apophatic tradition‚Äîknowing God beyond words.

‚è∞ Daily Office: Liturgy of the Hours

Sanctifying Time

The Prayer Rhythm

  • Lauds (Morning): Praise at sunrise
  • Terce, Sext, None (Midday): Brief pauses through the day
  • Vespers (Evening): Gratitude at sunset
  • Compline (Night): Surrender before sleep

Living Tradition

From Orthodox monasteries to Anglican parishes to modern prayer apps‚Äîmillions pray these hours together daily, joining the universal Church.

Creating Your Rule of Life

A Personal Spiritual Rhythm

What is a Rule of Life?

From the Latin "regula" (trellis)‚Äîa structure that supports spiritual growth. Not rigid law, but loving framework for flourishing.

Elements to Consider

  • Daily prayer rhythm (which practices, when?)
  • Weekly Sabbath rest
  • Monthly spiritual direction or retreat

  • Practices with your community
